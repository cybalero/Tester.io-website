Drawn, built,
undone.

Two cathedrals, two scrolls. From a line on paper to stone in the sun to geometry in the dark — first Barcelona, then Agra. Scroll to turn the page.

Plate I — The Blueprint
Antoni Gaudí · i. Cornet
Elevation · west façade
Act I

The Blueprint.

Gaudí drew it in 1882 — every column a catenary, every arch a calculation hung from a thread. A cathedral of lines before it was a cathedral of stone.

Act II

The Ascent.

Seven generations of masons. A century of scaffolding. Stone lifted one block at a time, each one cut to a drawing older than the mason holding it.

Act III

The Shadow.

At dusk the spires dissolve. The cathedral becomes geometry again — the same lines Gaudí drew, given back to the dark.

Plate Break · № 02

The Taj Mahal.

27° 10′ 30″ N 78° 02′ 31″ E Agra · Yamuna bank 1632 — 1653
Frame 001 / 120
Plate I — The Marble
Ustad Ahmad Lahori · architect
Elevation · south façade
Act I

The Marble.

Commissioned by Shah Jahan in 1632 — a mausoleum for Mumtaz, cut from ivory Makrana marble. Drawn in ink before a single stone was quarried.

Act II

The Inlay.

Twenty-two years. Twenty thousand artisans. Pietra Dura in lapis, jade, turquoise — every flower a cut gem laid flush with marble.

Act III

The Obsidian.

At dusk the marble turns to black. The inlay burns cyan. A monument to grief, re-rendered in electric light.
